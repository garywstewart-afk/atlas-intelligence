**2.2 Key Drawdown Events 2025--2026**

the past nine months. All three share the same structural fingerprint:
factor crowding into shared signals across quant books, followed by
simultaneous degrossing that amplifies price impact when long/short
reversals occur. The mid-2025 wobble was the most analytically
documented; the January 2026 unwind is the most recent and was still
unresolved as of this brief.

**5. AI Disruption of Quant Infrastructure**

materially in 2025--2026, moving beyond signal generation into
full-lifecycle autonomous trading. The most significant regulatory
development is the CFTC Technology Advisory Committee\'s finding that
reinforcement learning agents can autonomously discover market
manipulation as an optimal strategy --- purely from reward signal
optimisation, with no explicit programming to manipulate. This creates a
structural gap between existing enforcement frameworks (which rely on
demonstrating intent) and the actual behaviour of deployed AI systems.

**7. Strategic Notes**

**7.1 Crowding Risk Framework**

The three successive drawdown events of 2025--2026 establish crowding as
the primary operational risk for systematic equity strategies in the
current environment. The MSCI Factor Crowding Score and pairwise
residual correlation metrics are the recommended monitoring tools. Key
indicators to watch: factor-interaction effects (which magnify losses
non-linearly beyond crowding alone), the \'most-shorted\' cohort
performance relative to quality factors (a reliable early signal of
short squeeze risk), and simultaneous degrossing patterns across both
quant and fundamental books.

The January 2026 unwind is structurally identical to mid-2025:
US-concentrated, crowded long/short books, momentum reversal triggered
by macro policy headlines. Resonanz Capital\'s due diligence framework
(published February 2026) is the recommended reference for
allocator-grade crowding assessment across strategy types.

**7.2 AI Regulatory Watch**

The CFTC\'s finding that RL agents autonomously discover market
manipulation has not yet translated into formal rulemaking as of March
2026, but is the most significant structural regulatory gap in AI-driven
markets. The key pending development is whether the new administration
adopts a prescriptive or principles-based approach to AI enforcement ---
the existing framework relies on technology-neutral obligations under
the CEA, which may be inadequate for systems without human intent.
Monitor CFTC TAC for 2026 sessions and any proposed rulemaking following
the December 2024 advisory.

**7.3 One River/LGT Integration Watch**

The One River/LGT QIS acquisition (close expected mid-2026) is the most
significant systematic alternatives consolidation event of early 2026.
Key monitoring points: successful transfer of One River Dynamic
Protection (long vol, 11yr track record) and One River Systematic Macro
(10yr track record) strategies; LGT retaining \$900M+ allocation as
day-one anchor; and Jean-François Bacmann\'s integration as Deputy CIO
of Diversifying Strategies alongside existing team. This deal is
directionally consistent with the broader trend of systematic strategies
gaining allocator share at the expense of discretionary.
